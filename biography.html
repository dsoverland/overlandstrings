Biography

I was born in Duluth and received the best of my music education there. After graduation from UMD in 1956 I spent two years playing in Europe with the Seventh Army Symphony, a really first rate orchestra made up of young professional musicians drafted out of symphonies and universities across the USA. After leaving the Army I played one season with the Boston "Pops" under Arthur Fiedler before beginning my teaching career in Garden City, Kansas. After three years in Kansas I returned to Minnesota, married my wonderful wife Etta, and taught for two years in Rochester before moving to Moundsview to begin the string program in 1964. As you know, the Moundsview Orchestra Department has developed into one of the finest Orchestra Programs in the state due to its many fine teachers and a first class school system. In the mid 70's I graduated with a Master's Degree from the University of Minnesota.

After 30 years I retired from public school teaching but not from "music teaching." I have had as many as 32 private students but at the present teach 15 students weekly—although I think I could easily handle 20 students weekly.

During my years in Moundsview I have played, as a free lance musician, in the Saint Paul Chamber Orchestra, the "Golden Strings<" under Cliff Brunzell and many other venues in the Twin Cities area.

Doug Overland
